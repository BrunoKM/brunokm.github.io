I'm Bruno Mlodozeniec, a graduate deep learning researcher.

I'm currently a PhD student at the University of Cambridge with Richard Turner, David Krueger, and co-supervised by Bernhard Schölkopf. I'm broadly interested in understanding how intelligence and learning work, and how deep learning succeeds or fails at those. This has brought me to work on a range of topics from tools for interpreting neural networks (influence functions), generative modelling and symmetries, through to causality and probabilistic modelling.

I am also interested in AI for science, and have worked on applications to molecular dynamics simulation and modelling in synthetic biology.
